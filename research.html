Research

My research focuses on statistical inference for stochastic differential equations, with particular emphasis on multiscale systems, stochastic averaging, Bayesian inference, and machine-learning-based methods for learning effective dynamics.

I am especially interested in inverse problems where the observed slow dynamics are governed by an averaged or integral drift depending on an unknown latent distribution.

Research Interests

  • Stochastic differential equations
  • Stochastic averaging and multiscale dynamics
  • Bayesian inference for dynamical systems
  • Normalizing flows and variational inference
  • Machine learning methods for stochastic models

Current Projects

Learning multiscale stochastic models through normalizing flows.

Manuscript in preparation.

This project develops a Bayesian variational framework using normalizing flows to learn the dynamics of a multiscale stochastic dynamical system.

Neural Reversible-Jump MCMC for Integral-Drift Stochastic Differential Equations

Manuscript in preparation.

This project studies infinite dimensional Bayesian nonparametric inference for integral-drift stochastic differential equations using random discrete measures and reversible-jump Markov chain Monte Carlo methods.
